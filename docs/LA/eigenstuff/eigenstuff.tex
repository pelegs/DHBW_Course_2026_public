\section{Introduction}
Some lintear transformations (LTs) have unique vectors, which simply scale when the transformation is applied to them:
\begin{equation}
    T\left(\vvec{v}\right) = \lambda\vvec{v},\quad\lambda\in\mathbb{R}.
    \label{eq:eigen_eq_LT}
\end{equation}

In matrix form, if the matrix $\mtrx{A}$ represents $T$ in the standard basis set, then
\begin{equation}
    \mtrx{A}\vvec{v} = \lambda\vvec{v}.
    \label{eq:eigen_eq_matrix}
\end{equation}
(where $\lambda$ is the same scalar as in \autoref{eq:eigen_eq_LT})

We call such vectors the \newterm{Eigenvectors} of $\mtrx{A}$ with the corresponding scale factors $\lambda$ their respective \newterm{Eigenvalues}.

    $$ A = \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 0 \\ -3 & -1 \end{bmatrix}. $$
